Developed a decision tree model to predict customer likelihood of purchasing a car, using key features such as price, maintenance cost, safety ratings, and seating capacity. The model analyzes how these variables influence buying decisions by segmenting customers into distinct decision paths, making the results both interpretable and actionable.

Through training and validation, the model achieved an accuracy of 95%, with a low classification error rate of 10%, indicating strong predictive capability in identifying customer preferences. This high performance demonstrates the model’s effectiveness in capturing underlying patterns in the data and its potential usefulness for supporting targeted marketing strategies, product positioning, and customer segmentation.
