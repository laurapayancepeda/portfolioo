I’ve always been drawn to work that feels meaningful, work that contributes, to building a safer and more responsible world. That’s what made ComplyGuard’s mission resonate with me from the start.

ComplyGuard is a comprehensive B2G / B2C SaaS platform designed to support regulatory agencies in the gambling industry. It enables centralized monitoring of gaming operations, helping regulators streamline compliance, enforce legal standards, and gain real-time visibility through technology-driven systems.

Within this space, I work as a Data Analyst and Analytics Engineer, operating at the intersection of data, regulation, and real-world impact. My work spans across operational and revenue monitoring, tax compliance, responsible gaming and player behavior, KYC and anti-money laundering, and licensing. I also focus on automating complex reporting processes, data validation, and regulatory workflows to improve efficiency, consistency, and reliability.

I take ownership of the data pipeline from data extraction through to the final analytical layer. This involves transforming and modeling data into structured, reliable data that is ready for reporting, analysis, and decision-making. I build these systems with a focus on scalability, clarity, and long-term maintainability.

A key part of my role is understanding stakeholder needs and translating them into data models that reflect how businesses in the industry actually operate. I also ensure data quality through testing, validation, and clear documentation, so the outputs can be trusted and consistently used.

I often act as a bridge between technical teams and business users, ensuring that data is not only accurate, but also clear, accessible, and meaningful in practice.

At its core, my work is about bringing structure to complexity, turning raw, fragmented data into something dependable and genuinely useful. It’s about solving real challenges within the industry in a way that is efficient, practical, and grounded in its realities.
