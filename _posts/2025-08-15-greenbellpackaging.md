I’ve always loved small businesses. When I went to the UK for university, I ended up supporting loads of them, and that’s actually what inspired my thesis. My main focus was on helping SMEs better leverage technology that is often underutilised, typically due to constraints such as limited budgets, resource availability, and the fact that operational priorities often take precedence over structured data management.

During this period, I worked as a data analyst at Green Bell Packing, a B2B company specialising in sustainable packaging solutions for retail clients across the UK. I was responsible for delivering the end-to-end analytics workflow, developing a business intelligence solution that consolidated operational data into a usable decision-support system.

The company was using Odoo as its ERP/CRM system. I extracted and transformed this data, integrating it into Power BI to create a centralised reporting framework designed to improve visibility across key business functions and support daily operational decision-making.

I collaborated closely with stakeholders across sales, operations, and procurement to translate business requirements into structured analytics outputs, ensuring the dashboards were aligned with practical workflows rather than purely descriptive reporting.

**The solution was organised into seven functional reporting areas:**

**1. Supplier Performance Analysis**  
This section evaluated supplier reliability and efficiency, including on-time delivery performance, average lead times, order fulfilment status, and supplier-level comparison metrics to identify high and low-performing vendors.

**2. Purchase Order Analysis**  
Focused on procurement activity, this tab provided visibility into total purchase order values, order completion rates, currency distribution, and supplier-wise purchasing behaviour to support cost tracking and procurement planning.

**3. Inventory Insights**  
This module tracked stock availability and movement, including current stock levels, free-to-use versus allocated inventory, product demand trends, and identification of fast- and slow-moving items.

**4. Returns and Stock Movement Analysis**  
Designed to monitor stock adjustments and returns, this section analysed return frequency, reasons where available, product-level return patterns, and overall inventory movement trends over time.

**5. Sales Lead Performance**  
This tab focused on the early sales pipeline, tracking lead generation volumes, lead sources (where available), conversion ratios, and win/loss analysis to assess pipeline effectiveness.

**6. Quotation and Sales Conversion Analysis**  
This area examined the transition from quotation to closed sales, measuring quote conversion rates, customer engagement patterns, and drop-off points within the sales funnel.

**7. Sales Order Fulfilment Analysis**  
This final module tracked order execution performance, including fulfilment status, delivery completion rates, delays, and overall order processing efficiency.

In addition to dashboard development, I also acted in a consultative capacity, identifying inconsistencies within the ERP data structure and highlighting areas where system capabilities were underutilised. This included addressing data quality issues such as inconsistent categorisation, duplication across fields, and gaps in reporting logic, with the aim of improving overall data integrity and usability across the organisation.
