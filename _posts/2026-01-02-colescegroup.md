One thing about me is that I really enjoy expanding my experience. Whether it’s expanding the industries I work with, the types of data I work with, or different branches of data management, I’m open to all possibilities.

This time, I worked as a Client Reporting Analyst for The Coalesce Group, a B2B sales and lead generation company operating across the UK. I came in to support their weekly campaign reporting, which at the time was still being done manually while they were in the process of migrating to a more automated system.

I worked on weekly reporting for their campaigns, focusing on understanding key sales and funnel metrics, as well as reporting, validating, and ensuring figures were accurate for external stakeholders week to week. A big part of my role was making sure the reports were easy to understand, to the point, and included all the information and recommendations needed for decision-making.

Since the data was scattered across multiple sources, I spent time collecting and bringing it together, then calculating the key KPIs and metrics needed to support weekly decision-making. Although this was a short-term role driven by immediate business needs, it taught me a lot about the industry and the realities of week-to-week reporting, as well as how important it is to not just report data, but also provide context and recommendations that support decisions week to week
