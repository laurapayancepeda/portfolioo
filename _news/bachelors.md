During my undergraduate studies in International Business, I developed a broad and globally oriented understanding of how organisations operate across different economic, cultural, and regulatory environments. The programme combined core business disciplines such as marketing, finance, management, economics, and law with international perspectives, allowing me to understand how globalisation and cross-border dynamics shape modern business strategy and decision-making.

A key part of my academic journey involved building both analytical and communication skills, alongside developing intercultural awareness. Early modules introduced me to foundational concepts in organisations, management, and marketing, which helped me understand how businesses structure themselves and engage with customers in competitive markets. As my studies progressed, I explored more advanced topics such as international finance, corporate social responsibility, global strategic management, and international economic relations, which strengthened my ability to analyse business performance in a global context. I also developed an understanding of legal and ethical frameworks that influence international operations, as well as economic theories that explain firm behaviour and market dynamics.

In addition to business-focused learning, I enhanced my academic and professional skills through structured training in research, communication, and critical analysis. Language studies in Chinese and Arabic further supported my global outlook and cultural adaptability, enabling me to engage with diverse perspectives. Modules in psychological decision-making and knowledge management also deepened my understanding of how individuals and organisations make strategic choices and foster innovation. Overall, the programme provided a strong foundation in international business theory and practice, preparing me for further academic study and roles in global, data-informed, and strategically driven environments.

### Modules and Key Learnings

- **Fundamentals of Marketing**
  - Core marketing principles and consumer behaviour
  - Market segmentation and targeting strategies
  - Marketing mix and brand positioning

- **Accounting and Finance**
  - Basic financial accounting principles
  - Understanding financial statements
  - Introduction to corporate finance concepts

- **Global Capitalist Systems**
  - Economic systems and globalisation
  - Capital flows and international markets
  - Role of institutions in global economics

- **International Finance**
  - Foreign exchange and financial markets
  - Investment and risk in international contexts
  - Corporate financial decision-making

- **International Management**
  - Managing organisations across borders
  - Cross-cultural leadership and coordination
  - Global organisational strategy

- **International Business Law**
  - Legal frameworks in international trade
  - Contracts, regulations, and compliance
  - Risk and legal governance in global business

- **Corporate Social Responsibility**
  - Ethical business practices
  - Sustainability and stakeholder theory
  - Social and environmental accountability

- **Theory of the Firm**
  - Economic theories of firm behaviour
  - Production, cost, and market structures
  - Strategic decision-making foundations

- **International Development**
  - Economic development theories and global inequality
  - Role of institutions and policy in development
  - Business impact in emerging economies

- **International Economic Relations**
  - Trade theory and global economic policy
  - International institutions and agreements
  - Economic interdependence between countries

- **International Marketing**
  - Global marketing strategies and adaptation
  - Cross-cultural consumer behaviour
  - International branding and positioning

- **Psychological Approaches to Decision-Making**
  - Behavioural economics and cognitive biases
  - Individual and organisational decision processes
  - Risk perception and judgement

- **Strategic Management and Business Policy**
  - Corporate strategy formulation and implementation
  - Competitive analysis and industry structure
  - Long-term business planning

- **Business in Emerging Markets**
  - Market entry strategies in developing economies
  - Institutional and cultural challenges
  - Growth opportunities in emerging regions

- **Managing Knowledge for Innovation**
  - Knowledge creation and organisational learning
  - Innovation management and diffusion
  - Intellectual capital and competitive advantage

- **The Football Business**
  - Sports industry economics and governance
  - Commercialisation of football organisations
  - Media, branding, and revenue models in sport

- **Organisations and Management**
  - Organisational structures and behaviour
  - Management theories and leadership styles
  - Decision-making in business environments
